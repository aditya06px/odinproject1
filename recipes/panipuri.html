Pani-puri

Desription :

Panipuri consists of a round or ball-shaped, hollow puri (a deep-fried crisp flatbread), filled with a mixture of flavored water (known as imli pani), tamarind chutney, chili powder, chaat masala, potato mash, onion or chickpeas

Ingredients :

  • chaat masala
  • Fuchka
  • chili powde
  • potato mash

Steps :

  1. Assemble everything before you begin to make pani puri.
  2. Adding ice cubes may dilute the taste and flavor of the pani.
  3. Addition of black salt gives a really good taste and flavor. So if possible do try to add black salt in the pani recipe.
  4. Rinse 1 cup tightly packed coriander leaves, ½ cup tightly packed mint leaves, 2 to 3 green chilies and 1 inch ginger very well. Peel the ginger and roughly chop it.
  5. Add coriander leaves, mint leaves, ginger and green chili in a grinder or blender jar. For a less spicy pani you can add just 1 chopped green chilli.
  6. Now add 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder, 1 teaspoon chaat masala and salt as per taste.
  7. Add ⅓ cup water and grind to a smooth consistency.
  8. Remove the chutney in a bowl or a small pan.
  9. Now add ½ cup water in the grinder jar and swirl the jar so that the chutney at the sides of the jar gets mixed with the water.
  10. Then add ½ to ¾ cup water. You can add less or more water depending on the consistency you want.
  11. Mix very well. Cover the pan and refrigerate the pani. Before refrigerating you can also add 1 to 1.5 tablespoon boondi.
  12. Boil 2 to 3 medium potatoes. When warm, peel them and chop them in small cubes.
  13. Add 1 to 1.5 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves.
  14. Then add ¼ teaspoon red chili powder, 1 teaspoon chaat masala and 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder. Also add salt as per taste.
  15. Mix very well. Keep aside.
  16. Keep everything ready before you start assembling the pani puri.
